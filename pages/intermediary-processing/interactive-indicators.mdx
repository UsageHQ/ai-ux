## Solution

Implementing Interactive Indicators means going beyond static progress bars to include elements that users can interact with during AI operations. This approach involves:

- **Dynamic Progress Bars**: Creating progress bars that users can click on to see more detailed information about the processing stage or adjust the processing priority.
- **Pause/Resume Controls**: Allowing users to pause the AI operation, giving them the opportunity to modify inputs or settings before resuming.
- **Real-time Feedback Loop**: Enabling users to provide feedback or additional inputs in response to intermediate outputs or checkpoints, influencing the ongoing AI operation.

## Rationale

The rationale for incorporating Interactive Indicators in AI UX design is multifaceted, aiming to empower users and enhance their experience:

- **Enhancing User Agency**: Giving users some control over the AI processing empowers them, reducing feelings of frustration associated with passive waiting.
- **Maintaining Engagement**: Interactive elements keep users engaged with the application, potentially improving their satisfaction with the AI service.
- **Facilitating Transparency**: By allowing users to interact with the processing indicators, the AI system becomes more transparent, helping users understand the complexity and sophistication of the AI operations.
- **Enriching User Experience**: Interactive indicators can transform waiting time into an informative and interactive experience, contributing to a richer overall user journey.

Incorporating Interactive Indicators into the UX design of AI systems can significantly improve how users perceive and interact with AI processing stages. By fostering a more engaging, transparent, and user-controlled environment, designers can elevate the AI experience from one of passive waiting to active participation.
